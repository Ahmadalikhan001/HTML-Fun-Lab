What are Meta Tags?

Meta tags provide information about the webpage, but they are not displayed on the webpage itself.

They are written inside the section. Think of them as the ID card of your webpage. Real-Life Example Imagine you're applying for a university. The university receives: 📄 Your CV 📷 Your photo 🆔 Your passport 📞 Your phone number These documents describe you, but they are not you. Similarly, meta tags describe your webpage to browsers and search engines. Example My Website

Without viewport A mobile browser may shrink the entire desktop page, making text tiny. With viewport The page matches the device width and scales correctly. This is one of the first tags added to modern websites. 3. description Purpose Describes your webpage. Search engines may use this text below the page title in search results. 4. keywords Purpose Lists keywords related to the page. Note: Modern search engines like Google no longer use this tag for ranking, but you may still see it in older websites. 5. author Purpose Shows who created the webpage. 6. robots Purpose Gives instructions to search engine crawlers. Examples: index Allow the page to appear in search results. noindex Don't include the page in search results. follow Follow links on the page. nofollow Don't follow the links. 7. refresh Purpose Refreshes the page after 5 seconds. Or redirect to another page: After 5 seconds, the browser goes to the new page. Complete Example Learn HTML

Meta tags work the same way—they're mostly for browsers, search engines, and other software, not for visitors viewing the page.
